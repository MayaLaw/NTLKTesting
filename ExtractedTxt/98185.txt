Analysis 
The pilot reported that, during landing, the airplane bounced and then landed hard in a nose-down 
attitude. The propeller stuck the ground, and then the airplane slid and came to rest nose down. 
The airplane sustained substantial damage to the engine truss and mount. 
The pilot reported that there were no preaccident mechanical failures or malfunctions with the airplane 
that would have precluded normal operation.
Probable Cause and Findings
The National Transportation Safety Board determines the probable cause(s) of this accident to be:
The pilot's failure to maintain the proper pitch attitude during landing, which resulted in a bounced, hard 
landing.
