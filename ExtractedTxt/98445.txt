Analysis 
The pilot of the tailwheel-equipped airplane reported that, after landing, as he pressed the right rudder to 
turn off the runway, his foot slipped, he inadvertently applied the brake, and the airplane nosed over. 
The airplane sustained substantial damage to the right wing lift strut, left wing, and empennage.
The pilot reported that there were no preaccident mechanical failures or malfunctions with the airplane 
that would have precluded normal operation.
Probable Cause and Findings
The National Transportation Safety Board determines the probable cause(s) of this accident to be:
The pilot's inadvertent application of the brakes, which resulted in a nose-over.
