Analysis 
The solo student pilot reported that, after landing, he taxied the airplane to the ramp to get fuel. While in 
front of the fuel tank, he repositioned the airplane with a 360Â° turn. During a second attempt to 
reposition the airplane, the right wing struck a nearby pole.
The airplane sustained substantial damage to the right wing.
The student pilot reported that there were no preaccident mechanical failures or malfunctions with the 
airplane that would have precluded normal operation.
Probable Cause and Findings
The National Transportation Safety Board determines the probable cause(s) of this accident to be:
The student pilot's failure to maintain clearance from a pole while taxiing the airplane on the ramp.
