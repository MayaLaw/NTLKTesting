Analysis 
The solo student pilot reported that, after landing and while taxiing toward the fuel farm, he decided to 
maneuver through a partially sectioned-off area surrounded by orange construction cones. While he was 
focused on the cones, the airplane's right wing struck a concrete pole barrier surrounding the fuel farm. 
The airplane sustained substantial damage to the right wing and baggage door floorboards. 
The student reported that there were no preaccident mechanical failures or malfunctions with the 
airplane that would have precluded normal operation.
Probable Cause and Findings
The National Transportation Safety Board determines the probable cause(s) of this accident to be:
The student pilot's improper decision to maneuver the airplane through an area of construction and his 
subsequent failure to maintain clearance from a concrete pole barrier while taxiing.
