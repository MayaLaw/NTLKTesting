Analysis 
The student pilot reported that, during the landing roll of a solo flight, the airplane landed to the left of 
the runway centerline. He added that he applied right rudder but overcorrected and that the airplane 
veered to the right. The student pilot reported that he "panicked" and his leg "got stuck on the right 
rudder." The airplane veered off the runway to the right and impacted a ditch.
The airplane sustained substantial damage to the firewall.
The student pilot reported that there were no preaccident mechanical failures or malfunctions with the 
airplane that would have precluded normal operation.
Probable Cause and Findings
The National Transportation Safety Board determines the probable cause(s) of this accident to be:
The student pilot's rudder overcorrection during the landing roll, which resulted in a loss of directional 
control.
