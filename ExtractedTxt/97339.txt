Analysis 
The pilot of the tailwheel-equipped airplane reported that, during the landing roll, the airplane ground 
looped to the left.
The airplane sustained substantial damage to both wings and lift struts.
The pilot reported that there were no preaccident mechanical failures or malfunctions with the airplane 
that would have precluded normal operation.
The automated weather observation station located about 5 miles north of the accident airport reported 
that, about 7 minutes before the accident, the wind was from 220Â° at 7 knots. The airplane landed on 
runway 36.
Probable Cause and Findings
The National Transportation Safety Board determines the probable cause(s) of this accident to be:
The pilot's failure to maintain directional control during the landing roll with a tailwind.
