Analysis 
The pilot reported that, during landing, the airplane bounced. He initiated a go-around, and the airplane 
then veered left, touched back down on the ground, and impacted trees. 
The airplane sustained substantial damage to both wings.
The pilot reported that there were no preaccident mechanical failures or malfunctions with the airplane 
that would have precluded normal operation.
Probable Cause and Findings
The National Transportation Safety Board determines the probable cause(s) of this accident to be:
The pilot's failure to maintain yaw control during a go-around.
