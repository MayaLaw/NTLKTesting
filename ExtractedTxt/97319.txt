Analysis 
The pilot of the tailwheel-equipped airplane reported that, during landing on a grass airstrip, the airplane 
was close to an aerodynamic stall. He added that, during the landing, the airplane bounced multiple 
times, the propeller struck the ground, and the airplane then nosed over.
The airplane sustained substantial damage to the right wing lift strut.
The pilot reported that there were no preaccident mechanical failures or malfunctions with the airplane 
that would have precluded normal operation.
Probable Cause and Findings
The National Transportation Safety Board determines the probable cause(s) of this accident to be:
The pilot's improper landing flare, which resulted in a bounced landing.
