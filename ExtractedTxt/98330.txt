Analysis 
The pilot reported that, after completing an instructional flight, he dropped off the flight instructor and 
flew back to his home airport. During landing, the airplane porpoised, and the nose landing gear 
collapsed. 
The airplane sustained substantial damage to the lower fuselage.
The pilot reported that there were no mechanical malfunctions or failures with the airplane that would 
have precluded normal operation. 
Probable Cause and Findings
The National Transportation Safety Board determines the probable cause(s) of this accident to be:
The pilot's improper landing flare, which resulted in the airplane porpoising.
