Analysis 
The pilot of the tailwheel-equipped airplane reported that, after conducting a three-point landing, during 
the landing roll, the wind changed direction and lifted the left wing, and the airplane ground looped. 
The airplane sustained substantial damage to the right wing and elevator. 
The pilot reported that there were no preaccident mechanical failures or malfunctions with the airplane 
that would have precluded normal operation.
Probable Cause and Findings
The National Transportation Safety Board determines the probable cause(s) of this accident to be:
The pilot's failure to maintain directional control during landing, which resulted in a ground loop.
