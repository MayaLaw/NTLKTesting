Analysis 
The solo student pilot reported that, during departure and before rotation, the airplane veered left, and he 
applied opposite rudder, but the airplane impacted a taxiway sign. The nose landing gear separated from 
the firewall, and the airplane exited the runway to the left and came to rest upright in a field adjacent to 
the runway. 
The airplane sustained substantial damage to the firewall structural plate and the lower firewall flange.  
The student reported that there were no preaccident mechanical failures or malfunctions with the 
airplane that would have precluded normal operation.
Probable Cause and Findings
The National Transportation Safety Board determines the probable cause(s) of this accident to be:
The student pilot's failure to maintain directional control during takeoff.
