Analysis 
The pilot reported that, after landing on a gravel airstrip, during the taxi, a moose cow and calf ran onto 
the airstrip, moving from the pilot's right to left. She added that she veered to the left to avoid the 
animals and the airplane struck a tree.
The airplane sustained substantial damage to the fuselage.
The pilot reported that there were no preaccident mechanical failures or malfunctions with the airplane 
that would have precluded normal operation.
Probable Cause and Findings
The National Transportation Safety Board determines the probable cause(s) of this accident to be:
The pilot's abrupt maneuver to avoid a moose cow and calf during taxi, which resulted in an impact with 
a tree.
