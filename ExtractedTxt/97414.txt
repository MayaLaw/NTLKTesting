Analysis 
The pilot reported that, during short final at night, he reduced power to idle, and the airplane descended 
rapidly and landed hard in a flat attitude. He added that, after touchdown, the airplane bounced, and he 
applied full power to go around. He entered the traffic pattern and then landed without further incident.
Postaccident examination 3 days after the accident revealed that the airplane sustained substantial 
damage to the fuselage.
The pilot reported that there were no preaccident mechanical failures or malfunctions with the airplane 
that would have precluded normal operation.
Probable Cause and Findings
The National Transportation Safety Board determines the probable cause(s) of this accident to be:
The pilot's improper landing flare, which resulted in a hard landing.
