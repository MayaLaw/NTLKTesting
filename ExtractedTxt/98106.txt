Analysis 
The pilot reported that, during the takeoff roll to the southeast on a private airstrip, about 120 ft down 
the runway, the left wing struck some tree foliage. The airplane veered left, exited the runway, and 
impacted trees. He added that he was watching the right side of the runway for a swale and was not 
paying attention to the left side of the runway.
The airplane sustained substantial damage to the left wing.
The pilot reported that there were no preaccident mechanical failures or malfunctions with the airplane 
that would have precluded normal operation.
Probable Cause and Findings
The National Transportation Safety Board determines the probable cause(s) of this accident to be:
The pilot's failure to maintain clearance from tree foliage on the left side of the runway during takeoff 
due to his focus on the right side of the runway.
