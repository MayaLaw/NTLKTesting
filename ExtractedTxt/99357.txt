Analysis 
The pilot of a tailwheel-equipped airplane reported that, while landing on a grass runway, he applied the 
brakes and retracted the flaps and that the airplane subsequently nosed over. 
The airplane sustained substantial damage to the left wing and vertical stabilizer.
The pilot reported that there were no preaccident mechanical failures or malfunctions with the airplane 
that would have precluded normal operation.
Probable Cause and Findings
The National Transportation Safety Board determines the probable cause(s) of this accident to be:
The pilot's overapplication of the brakes during landing on a grass runway, which resulted in a nose-
over.
