Analysis 
The pilot of the tailwheel-equipped airplane reported that, during the landing roll about 40 knots ground 
speed, the airplane drifted left, and he corrected back to the runway center. However, the airplane then 
drifted right, and he was unable to correct back to the runway center. The airplane ground looped to the 
right, and the left wing struck the ground before the airplane came to a stop.
The airplane sustained substantial damage to the forward lower fuselage bulkhead and the left wing. 
The pilot reported that there were no mechanical malfunctions or failures with the airplane that would 
have precluded normal operation. 
Probable Cause and Findings
The National Transportation Safety Board determines the probable cause(s) of this accident to be:
The pilot's failure to maintain directional control during the landing roll.
