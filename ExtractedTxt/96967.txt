Analysis 
The flight instructor reported that, during landing, while the student pilot was flying, the airplane 
ballooned. He added that he took the flight controls and landed on the runway. 
The airplane sustained substantial damage to the left wing.
The flight instructor reported that there were no preaccident mechanical failures or malfunctions with 
the airplane that would have precluded normal operation.
Probable Cause and Findings
The National Transportation Safety Board determines the probable cause(s) of this accident to be:
The flight instructor's improper landing flare, which resulted in a hard landing.
