Analysis 
After landing and while taxiing to the hangar, the bottom of the airplane's fuselage began to drag on the 
ground. Realizing that something was wrong, the pilot stopped the airplane and turned the engine 
magneto switch to off. Postaccident examination revealed that a fatigue failure of the main landing gear 
(MLG) truss had occurred, which allowed the MLG to spread apart. 
 
 
Probable Cause and Findings
The National Transportation Safety Board determines the probable cause(s) of this accident to be:
The fatigue failure of the main landing gear (MLG) truss, which resulted in the MLG spreading apart.
