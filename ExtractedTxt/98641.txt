Analysis 
The pilot reported that, during the landing flare, the airplane suddenly rolled left. He quickly corrected, 
landed, and then taxied to the ramp without further incident. He conducted a postflight inspection and 
found that the left wing tip had struck the runway.
The airplane sustained substantial damage to the left aileron. 
The pilot reported that there were no preaccident mechanical failures or malfunctions with the airplane 
that would have precluded normal operation.
Probable Cause and Findings
The National Transportation Safety Board determines the probable cause(s) of this accident to be:
The pilot's failure to maintain lateral control of the airplane during landing, which resulted in a wing 
strike.
