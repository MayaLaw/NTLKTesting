Analysis 
The pilot reported that during landing, about 20 ft above ground level and about 80 kts, he reached for 
his iPad. He took his eyes off the runway to grab the device that was located on the seat next to him, and 
the airplane collided with the ground in a nose-down attitude. The pilot reported that he should not have 
taken his eyes off the runway during landing.
The airplane sustained substantial damage to the lower fuselage stringers, longerons, and formers.
The pilot reported that there were no mechanical malfunctions or failures with the airplane that would 
have precluded normal operation. 
Probable Cause and Findings
The National Transportation Safety Board determines the probable cause(s) of this accident to be:
The pilot's unnecessary action during the approach, which resulted in a hard landing.
