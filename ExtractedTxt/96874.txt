Analysis 
The pilot reported that, during touchdown in crosswind conditions, it "felt like [the airplane] landed 
heavily on the nose wheel." He added that he taxied to park without further incident.
Postaccident examination revealed that the airplane sustained substantial damage to the fuselage.
The pilot reported that there were no preaccident mechanical failures or malfunctions with the airplane 
that would have precluded normal operation.
The automated weather observation system located at the accident airport reported that, about the time

The National Transportation Safety Board determines the probable cause(s) of this accident to be:
The pilot's improper landing flare, which resulted in substantial damage.
